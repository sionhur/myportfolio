Memories of Landscapes

Plywood, Steel, Fabric, Lightbulb, Electric Motor
April, 2022
18" x 24"
See More
On a particularly exhausting day, I find a moment of rest by looking out the window on the bus.
Moving images of landscapes and skies recall memories of places I’ve been before.
​​​​​​​When new things would wear me out, I would revisit old places, tracing them in the shapes and colors left by the sunlight.
The clear skies, the clouds, the rain, the snow, old buildings, skyscrapers, mountains, and rivers
all pass by in new forms every day that somehow appear familiar at times.
These images are reflected in my sculpture, with obvious and abstract traces of shapes and colors that resemble landscapes in our memories.
The inner cylinder and the outer cylinder move in opposite directions creating new landscapes shining through the translucent fabric,
just like the way landscapes change when we look out the window on a running bus.
