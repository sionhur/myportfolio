After the Visit

Metal, Lightbulb, Dyed string, Paint
November 2019
See More
When I lived in Korea, I remember being surrounded by these large masses of mountains casting shadows
on everything on earth, more and more as the sun moved down.
The farther the mountains were from me, the hazier, bluer, and grayer they seemed to become.
As endless as they were, what was left to me in memory were mere undulations formed by the outlines of the mountain range.
Then, the shades of the sky are immersed into these masses of land, in a freely moving, wild nature.
As the colors of the light in the sky fade into each other, the mountain range smoothly rises and falls.
